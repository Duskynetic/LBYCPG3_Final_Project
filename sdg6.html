SDG 6 Global Acceleration Framework

The SDG 6 Global Acceleration Framework is an UN-proposed plan that aims to accelerate the attainment of SDG 6. This calls for the cooperation of UN agencies, governments, civil society, private sectors and stakeholders to support countries from 2020 to 2030 to achieve the targets related to the SDG through the accelerators defined by the UN:

  1. Financing: Optimized financing is essential to get resources behind country plans.
  2. Data and information: Data and information targets resources and measures progress.
  3. Capacity development: A better-skilled workforce improves service levels and increases job creation and retention in the water sector.
  4. Innovation: New, smart practices and technologies will improve water and sanitation resources management and service delivery.
  5. Governance: Collaboration across boundaries and sectors will make SDG 6 everyone's business.

New Centennial Water Source-Kaliwa Dam Project

The Kaliwa Dam Project aims to construct a new water source for Metro Manila, Rizal, and Quezon given the increasing demand of water in the said regions wherein 17.46 million people or 3.49 million households reside. This project is led by the Metropolitan Waterworks and Sewerage System to be started in June 2022 and finished by August 2026.

The dam is a 60m high and 291 hectares wide located in Quezon province which has a projected capability to supply 600 million liters of water per day. This project responds to the UN SDG 6 which aims to provide everyone access to clean water supply.

Targets and Indicators

  1. By 2030, achieve universal and equitable access to safe and affordable drinking water for all
    1. Proportion of population using safely managed drinking water services
  2. By 2030, achieve access to adequate and equitable sanitation and hygiene for all and end open defecation, paying special attention to the needs of women and girls and those in vulnerable situations
    1. Proportion of population using (a) safely managed sanitation services and (b) a hand-washing facility with soap and water
  3. By 2030, improve water quality by reducing pollution, eliminating dumping and minimizing release of hazardous chemicals and materials, halving the proportion of untreated wastewater and substantially increasing recycling and safe reuse globally
    1. Proportion of domestic and industrial wastewater flows safely treated
    2. Proportion of bodies of water with good ambient water quality
  4. By 2030, substantially increase water-use efficiency across all sectors and ensure sustainable withdrawals and supply of freshwater to address water scarcity and substantially reduce the number of people suffering from water scarcity
    1. Change in water-use efficiency over time
    2. Level of water stress: freshwater withdrawal as a proportion of available freshwater
  5. By 2030, implement integrated water resources management at all levels, including through transboundary cooperation as appropriate
    1. Degree of integrated water resources management
    2. Proportion of transboundary basin area with an operational arrangement for water
  6. By 2020, protect and restore water-related ecosystems, including mountains, forests, wetlands, rivers, aquifers and lakes
    1. Change in the extent of water-related ecosystems over time
  7. By 2030, expand international cooperation and capacity-building support to developing countries in water- and sanitation-related activities and programmes, including water harvesting, desalination, water efficiency, wastewater treatment, recycling and reuse technologies
    1. Amount of water- and sanitation-related official development assistance that is part of a government-coordinated spending plan
  8. Support and strengthen the participation of local communities in improving water and sanitation management
    1. Proportion of local administrative units with established and operational policies and procedures for participation of local communities in water and sanitation management

Progress Updates

Lifted from the Report of the Secretary -General, Progress towards the Sustainable Development Goals-E/2022:

  • 2015 - 2020
    • 74% (+4%) of the world population has access to safely managed drinking water services
    • 54% (+7%) of the world population experience safely managed sanitation
    • 71% (+4%) of the world population has access to household handwashing facilities with soap and water
  • 2019
    • 12% increase in water use efficiency
  • 2020
    • 60% of rivers, lakes, and aquifers assessed to have good water quality in 97 countries
  • 153 countries collectively and peacefully manage and share transboundary rivers in response to climate change.
