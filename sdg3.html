Human Resources Health (HRH) Program

The government's initiative to deploy, redistribute, and retain health professionals across the nation to improve access to high-quality medical care is the Human Resource for Health (HRH) Program of the Department of Health (DOH). Far-flung, isolated, underserved, and remote towns with insufficient medical staff are priority regions.

The DOH HRH Program would receive a total financing of P17 billion, it was announced during a Senate hearing for the national budget for 2022. 23 364 health professionals, including nurses, doctors, midwives, medical technologists, dentists, physical therapists, pharmacists, and nutritionists, will be hired and deployed using this.

Health Facilities Enhancement Program (HFEP)

By building new public health facilities, upgrading and renovating the ones that already exist, like barangay health stations, rural health units/urban health centers, and local government unit-run hospitals, HFEP seeks to strengthen public health facilities throughout the nation.

Once it is up and running, the project in the image above, known as the Naga City Super Health Center (NCSHC), would offer basic healthcare requirements as well as localized specialist healthcare services. Work is anticipated to be finished in the first quarter of 2023.
