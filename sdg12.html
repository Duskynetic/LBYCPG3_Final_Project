Philippine Action Plan for Sustainable Consumption and Production (PAP4SCP)

By implementing programmatic policy reforms and a set of actions over the short- (2020-2022), medium- (2022-2030), and long-terms, the Philippine Action Plan for SCP (PAP4SCP) will act as a framework for influencing and directing sustainable behavior and practices across sectors and levels of government (2030-2040).

The PAP4SCP will help the nation achieve Ambisyon Natin 2040 so that the policies and initiatives put in place would guarantee that Filipinos, both in the current and future generations, experience a "matatag (strongly rooted), maginhawa (comfortable), and panatag na buhay (secure life)." This will become a reality when more Filipinos adopt sustainable and climate-smart activities and lifestyles and increase their use of green products and services.

Establishment of the LA-ICP-MS Facility

The DENR-SMARTSeas BMB's PH Project was created to hasten the creation of marine protected areas (MPAs) and MPA Networks in order to slow and stop the rapid deterioration of marine and coastal habitats. SMARTSeas Philippines currently has five (5) project sites with the following characteristics: Batangas-Mindoro (with the highest shore fish biodiversity and eight rare coral species), Palawan (58,400 hectares or 42 percent of the nation's mangroves), Taxon Strait (with 14 species of cetaceans), Surigao-Siargao (with hawksbill and green sea turtles), and Davao Gulf (feeding ground of 7 species of whales and 4 species of dolphins). To "lower pollution level against baseline levels" at these locations is one of SMARTSeas PH's goals.

Clean Energy Alert

This DOST-National Research Council of the Philippines (NRCP) program's objective is to look into alternate energy sources in the country. The obstacle is obvious and intimidating. Over the next 20 years, it is anticipated that the energy needs of our nation will double as it continues to industrialize and modernize. Meeting these demands while attempting to slow the rate of increasing greenhouse gas emissions (GHGs) that cause climate change is a challenge. Therefore, it is crucial to conduct research on cleaner forms of energy. Above all, this program will demonstrate how renewable energy can promote national energy independence while saving the government money, creating employment and wealth for the nation. It can also provide access to energy for the most vulnerable residents of underserved communities.

This program's overarching goal is to look into and examine alternative energy sources in the Philippines that can be used to meet the country's increasing energy demand. Explorations in this area help progress the life, physical, and engineering sciences as well as other disciplines. The studies carried out as part of this program will lay the groundwork for scientific advancement and discovery that will promote industrial growth, create jobs and wealth for the nation, increase access to energy for the most vulnerable residents of underserved communities, and promote national energy independence.
