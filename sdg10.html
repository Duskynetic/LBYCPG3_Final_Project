Universal Access to Tertiary Education

In addition to establishing the tertiary education subsidy and student loan program, the Universal Access to Quality Tertiary Education Act (RA 10931), which was signed into law last August 2017, ensures free tuition and other school fees in state universities and colleges, local universities and colleges, and state-run technical-vocational institutions in the Philippines. It also strengthens the Unified Student Financial Assistance System for Tertiary Education.

Mandatory PhilHealth Coverage for Senior Citizens

2014 saw the approval of RA No. 10645, or an Act Providing for the Mandatory Philhealth Coverage for All Senior Citizens. The national health insurance program of PhilHealth is required by law to provide coverage for all senior persons. The National Health Insurance Fund of PhilHealth, which receives its funding from the earnings of Republic Act No. 10351, often known as the Sin Tax Law, would be used to assure the enrolment of all older residents who are not already covered by any existing category.

Social Pension Program for Indigent Senior Citizens

The DSWD's SPISC program, which offers additional government aid, pays older adults in need a monthly stipend of PhP 500 to supplement their daily necessities and other medical requirements. The program aims to enhance the daily sustenance and medical needs of elderly people who are in need, decrease the prevalence of hunger, and safeguard them from abuse, neglect, deprivation, and natural and man-made calamities.

SPISC is in line with SDG 1's target 1.1, which states that extreme poverty, currently defined as living on less than $1.25 per day, must be eradicated for all people worldwide by 2030; target 1.2 states that men, women, and children of all ages must live in poverty in at least half of its dimensions by 2030; and target 1.3 states that all people must have access to social protection systems and measures, including floors, by 2030.
