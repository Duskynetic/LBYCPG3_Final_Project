The Green Voyage 2050

Last May 2019, the International Maritime Organization and the government of Norway launched the GreenVoyage2050 Project in response to UN SDGs 5, 7, 9, 13, 14, and 17. This project aims to introduce and implement energy efficiency measures in the shipping industry to effectively lessen its contribution to the global carbon footprint.

The project prioritizes supporting developing countries, small island developing states, and least developed countries. Overall, this project is focused on developing, testing, and promoting zero and low-emission technology for the shipping industry. In other words, it advocates the clean and sustainable use of energy in the maritime sector.

The Green Energy Option Program (GEOP)

The Independent Electricity Market Operator of the Philippines (IEMOP) started the Green Energy Option Program in the Philippines on December 3, 2021. The launching of this project is in compliance with the Energy Act of 2008 focused on developing renewable energy technologies in the Philippines. This is also a response to UN SDG 7. In this program, Filipinos are given the opportunity to utilize renewable energy resources such as biomass, solar, wind, geothermal, ocean energy, and hydropower as a source of their electricity rather than the traditional one which is from burning fossil fuels.

The Department of Energy provides a list of authorized renewable energy suppliers that the consumers can pick from. However, this program is still limited to consumers with an average peak electricity requirement equal to or greater than 100kW. Indeed, this program promotes the use of clean and sustainable energy that will lessen the carbon footprints from energy production.

National Renewable Energy Program

The Department of Energy updated the National Renewable Energy Program (NREP) for 2020-2040 last November 13, 2021. This program was originally launched last 2011. This aims to increase the contribution of renewable energy in the power generation mix in the country to 35% by 2030 and 50% by 2040.

The NREP is a response to the Renewable Act of 2008 an SDG 7 by setting the roadmap for achieving renewable energy goals in the country and developing and adopting renewable energy technologies. By shifting to renewable sources of energy, the country would be able to provide sufficient, sustainable, and cheaper energy to its people and help lessen the carbon footprints from energy production which would help prevent climate change from worsening.

Targets and Indicators

  1. By 2030, ensure universal access to affordable, reliable and modern energy services
    1. Proportion of population with access to electricity
    2. Proportion of population with primary reliance on clean fuels and technology
  2. By 2030, increase substantially the share of renewable energy in the global energy mix
    1. Renewable energy share in the total final energy consumption
  3. By 2030, double the global rate of improvement in energy efficiency
    1. Energy intensity measured in terms of primary energy and GDP
  4. By 2030, enhance international cooperation to facilitate access to clean energy research and technology, including renewable energy, energy efficiency and advanced and cleaner fossil-fuel technology, and promote investment in energy infrastructure and clean energy technology
    1. International financial flows to developing countries in support of clean energy research and development and renewable energy production, including in hybrid systems
  5. By 2030, expand infrastructure and upgrade technology for supplying modern and sustainable energy services for all in developing countries, in particular least developed countries, small island developing States, and land-locked developing countries, in accordance with their respective programmes of support
    1. Installed renewable energy-generating capacity in developing countries (in watts per capita)
