Making Cities Resilient 2030 (MCR2030)

MCR2030 is a distinctive cross-stakeholder initiative that aims to increase local resilience through advocacy, knowledge and experience exchange, the creation of mutually reinforcing city-to-city learning networks, the addition of technical expertise, the linking of various levels of government, and the development of partnerships. Among its core partners are R-Cities, C40 Cities, ICLEI, the international federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies, JICA, the United Cities and Local Governments, the UN Human Settlements Programme, UNDRR, the UN Office for Project Services, The World Bank Group, and the World Council on City Data. It is a global partnership of experts in urban resilience, DRR, climate change, and the SDGs.

The main strategic goals of the MCR2030 include raising city risk awareness and commitments to disaster risk reduction and resilience, raising city capacities to plan for risk reduction and resilience, and raising city capacities to implement resilience, with a view to assisting cities in working towards achieving the Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction, the New Urban Agenda, the Paris Agreement, and the Sustainable Development Goals by 2030.

Implementation of Toxic Substances and Hazardous Waste Management

This program of the DENR-EMB addresses the sound and proper management of industrial chemicals and hazardous wastes in all aspects of their life cycle, from importation, manufacture, distribution, transportation, use, and disposal. It does so in accordance with RA 6969 Toxic Substances and Hazardous and Nuclear Waste Control Act of 1990. By significantly reducing their release to air, water, and soil, this program will help achieve target 12.4: achieve environmentally sound management of chemicals and all wastes throughout their life cycles, in accordance with accepted international frameworks, and reduce their negative effects on human health and the environment.

LGU Assistance for Comprehensive Land-Use

Executive Order (EO) No. 72 mandates the creation and implementation of LGUs' Comprehensive Land Use Plans (CLUPs) in accordance with the Local Government Code of 1991 to ensure sensible land use and sustainable urban and regional development. In accordance with their territorial jurisdiction, CLUPs specify the uses of land and other physical and natural resources, both public and private, including co-managed areas with the national government and, as necessary, management plans for ancestral domains, crucial watersheds, river basins, and protected areas.

In addition to reviewing and approving provincial physical framework plans, the Housing and Land Use Regulatory Board (HLURB) I offers technical assistance to local government units (LGUs), highly urbanized cities (HUCs), and independent component cities (ICC) in the formulation and updating of their CLUPs (PPFPs). Out of the 1,634 LGUs, 1,569, or 96 percent, have CLUPs approved as of 2017.
