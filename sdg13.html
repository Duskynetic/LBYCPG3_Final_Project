Survival & Recovery Assistance Program

The Survival and Recovery (SURE) Assistance Program of the DA-Agricultural Credit Policy Council seeks to support the government's objective of assisting agricultural households in disaster-affected areas regain their capacity to earn a living, as well as to offer immediate relief to small farmers and fisherfolk through loan and grant assistance. Farmers and fishermen's livelihoods will return to normal thanks to DA's SURE Loan Program, which will assist them ease the strain of being left on their own and recover from their losses.

By lowering susceptibility to disasters and the accompanying economic shock, the initiative will assist in enhancing the resilience of the underprivileged and those in vulnerable circumstances.

Weather Research and Forecasting

Providing Weather Research and Forecasting (WRF) Model-based High Resolution (5 km) Climate Change Projections for the Philippines. Using the RCP4.5 and RCP8.5 climate change scenarios, this project by the DOST-PAGASA intends to give a high-resolution (5 km) climate change prediction using the WRF model, covering the entire Philippines. The system's outputs are anticipated to help the LGUs update their CC-DRR-enhanced local plans (i.e., CLUP, LCCAP).

By delivering timely and accurate disaster and climate information that will promote risk-informed planning, particularly at the local level, this project is projected to help increase the resilience and adaptive ability of communities and ecosystems to climate-related hazards and natural catastrophes (SDG13.1).

Targets and Indicators

  1. Strengthen resilience and adaptive capacity to climate-related hazards and natural disasters in all countries
    1. Number of deaths, missing persons and directly affected persons attributed to disasters per 100,000 population
    2. Number of countries that adopt and implement national disaster risk reduction strategies in line with the Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction 2015-2030
    3. Proportion of local governments that adopt and implement local disaster risk reduction strategies in line with national disaster risk reduction strategies
  2. Integrate climate change measures into national policies, strategies and planning
    1. Number of countries with nationally determined contributions, long-term strategies, national adaptation plans and adaptation communications, as reported to the secretariat of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change
    2. Total greenhouse gas emissions per year
  3. Improve education, awareness-raising and human and institutional capacity on climate change mitigation, adaptation, impact reduction and early warning
    1. Extent to which (i) global citizenship education and (ii) education for sustainable development are mainstreamed in (a) national education policies; (b) curricula; (c) teacher education; and (d) student assessment
  4. Implement the commitment undertaken by developed-country parties to the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change to a goal of mobilizing jointly $100 billion annually by 2020 from all sources to address the needs of developing countries in the context of meaningful mitigation actions and transparency on implementation and fully operationalize the Green Climate Fund through its capitalization as soon as possible
    1. Amounts provided and mobilized in United States dollars per year in relation to the continued existing collective mobilization goal of the $100 billion commitment through to 2025
  5. Promote mechanisms for raising capacity for effective climate change-related planning and management in least developed countries and small island developing States, including focusing on women, youth and local and marginalized communities
    1. Number of least developed countries and small island developing States with nationally determined contributions, long-term strategies, national adaptation plans and adaptation communications, as reported to the secretariat of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change
