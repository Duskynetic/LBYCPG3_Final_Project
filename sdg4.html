Universal Access to Tertiary Education

De Vera claims that the law's enactment enabled 100 local government-established universities, 112 universities and colleges, and public universities to start offering educational programs in 2018, giving young Filipinos access to "excellent education." In 2019, more than 200 public institutions in the Philippines' 1.19 million students quit paying their tuition and other fees.

The DOH HRH Program would receive a total financing of P17 billion, it was announced during a Senate hearing for the national budget for 2022. 23 364 health professionals, including nurses, doctors, midwives, medical technologists, dentists, physical therapists, pharmacists, and nutritionists, will be hired and deployed using this.

Free Technical Vocational Educational & Training Programs

Under the TVET programs, there are center-based programs, community-based programs, enterprise based programs, school-based programs, as well as online programs. From the government association, Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA), offers free services to Filipinos who would want to enhance their skills.
